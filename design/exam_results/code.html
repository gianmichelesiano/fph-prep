Question 14 — Phytotherapy
close INCORRECT

Which of the following is the primary contraindication for the use of Hypericum perforatum extract in a patient currently undergoing anticoagulation therapy with Phenprocoumon?

YOUR ANSWER

Potential for increased bleeding due to anti-platelet effects of Hypericin.

CORRECT ANSWER

Induction of CYP3A4 leading to decreased therapeutic levels of Phenprocoumon.

info Clinical Rationale

St. John's Wort (Hypericum perforatum) is a potent inducer of hepatic enzymes, specifically CYP3A4, CYP2C9, and the transport protein P-glycoprotein. Since Phenprocoumon is metabolized via these pathways, the induction leads to rapid clearance of the anticoagulant, reducing the INR and increasing the risk of thrombotic events. It is NOT an anti-platelet interaction.

Question 22 — Clinical Pharmacy
check CORRECT

A 68-year-old male presents with chronic kidney disease (eGFR 35 mL/min). Which dose adjustment is required for Metformin according to Swiss FPH guidelines?

SELECTED ANSWER

Maximum dose should not exceed 1000mg daily.

info Clinical Rationale

In patients with Stage 3b CKD (eGFR 30–44 mL/min), Metformin can be continued but at a reduced maximum dose of 1000mg per day to avoid lactic acidosis. If eGFR falls below 30 mL/min, the medication must be discontinued immediately.
